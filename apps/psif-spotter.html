Why pSIF Identification is Critical

Most fatalities are preceded by near-misses. The question is: did anyone recognize them?

The Iceberg Problem

For every fatality, there are hundreds of near-misses with the same potential. Most go unrecognized and unreported.

Outcome Bias

We judge incidents by what happened, not what could have happened. A minor injury gets minor attention — even when it was nearly fatal.

Trainable Skill

pSIF identification isn't intuition — it's a skill that can be developed through practice. That's exactly what this app does.

Example Scenarios

Real situations. Real learning.

Mining pSIF: YES

A haul truck reversed close to a light vehicle. The LV driver saw it coming and moved forward. No contact was made.

Why it's a pSIF:

The energy differential between a haul truck and light vehicle is extreme. The only thing preventing a fatality was the LV driver's awareness. Under different circumstances (blind spot, fatigue, distraction), this would have been fatal.

Construction pSIF: NO

Worker stepped on a nail that penetrated their work boot. First aid treatment only. Worker returned to work next day.

Why it's NOT a pSIF:

While painful, the energy involved (nail penetration) has limited severity potential. Even under worse circumstances (deeper penetration, infection), serious injury or fatality is unlikely. This is a standard housekeeping issue.
