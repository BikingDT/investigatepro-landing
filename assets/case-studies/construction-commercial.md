# Case Study: Commercial Construction Company

## How a Top-25 Commercial Builder Passed OSHA Inspection and Improved Their TRIR by 35%

**Industry:** Commercial Construction  
**Company Size:** 2,400 employees, $1.2B annual revenue  
**Region:** Southeast United States (FL, GA, NC, SC)  

---

## The Challenge

A leading commercial construction company faced mounting pressure from multiple directions: increasing OSHA scrutiny, rising insurance premiums, and client requirements for stronger safety records.

### The Problems:

**OSHA Pressure**
- Two citations in previous 12 months
- Pending inspection at flagship project
- "Willful violation" warning from previous incident

**Inconsistent Investigation Process**
- Project managers conducted investigations (no dedicated safety)
- Quality varied dramatically by site
- No standardized methodology

**Paper-Based Chaos**
- Investigations stored in project trailers
- No central visibility
- Impossible to identify patterns

**Client Requirements**
- Major clients requiring EMR < 1.0
- Lost two bids due to safety record
- Prequalification increasingly focused on investigation quality

> "We were one bad incident away from losing our largest client. And we had no system to prevent it."
> 
> — **Director of Risk Management**

---

## The Solution

The company implemented InvestigatePro as part of a comprehensive safety improvement initiative, focusing on investigation quality as a leading indicator.

### Implementation Approach:

**Phase 1: Assessment (2 weeks)**
- Reviewed 50 historical investigations
- Identified gaps in methodology
- Mapped current workflow

**Phase 2: Rapid Deployment (4 weeks)**
- Deployed to 15 active project sites
- Mobile-first rollout (field usability critical)
- Trained 45 supervisors on ICAM basics

**Phase 3: Integration (4 weeks)**
- Connected to existing safety management system
- Established corporate review workflow
- Built custom templates for construction-specific hazards

### Key Features Utilized:

✅ **Mobile Data Collection** — Field supervisors could start investigations immediately  
✅ **Photo/Video Evidence** — Visual documentation built into workflow  
✅ **AI Investigation Coaching** — Guided even non-safety professionals to better findings  
✅ **Corrective Action Tracking** — Automatic reminders and escalation  
✅ **Client Reporting** — Professional investigation summaries for owner requirements

## Key Learnings

### What Made It Work:

1. **Field-First Design**
   - Mobile app worked offline in concrete structures
   - Photo capture reduced documentation time
   - Supervisors could complete 80% of investigation on-site

2. **Non-Expert Users**
   - Construction supervisors aren't safety professionals
   - AI coaching guided them through ICAM methodology
   - Quality was consistent regardless of who investigated

3. **Speed Created Buy-In**
   - Old process: 21 days, endless revisions
   - New process: 6 days, first-time approval
   - Supervisors became advocates (less paperwork)

4. **Visibility Changed Behavior**
   - Leadership dashboard showed investigation quality scores
   - Sites competed on metrics (gamification effect)
   - Poor investigations became visible immediately

### What They'd Do Differently:

> "We underestimated how much near-miss reporting would increase. Once people saw that investigations led to real changes — and nobody got blamed — they started reporting everything. We had to staff up to keep up with the volume."
>
> — **VP of Operations**

---

## The Organizational Factors They Found

Three systemic issues emerged from cross-project analysis:

**Subcontractor Management**
- 60% of incidents involved subcontractors
- Pre-qualification focused on price, not safety
- Implemented safety scoring in bid evaluation

**Tool & Equipment**
- Specific scaffold brand involved in 4 fall-related incidents
- Manufacturer defect identified through pattern analysis
- Initiated industry-wide recall collaboration

**Schedule Pressure**
- 70% of incidents occurred in final 2 weeks before milestones
- "Push" culture created risk-taking
- Revised scheduling buffers and milestone penalties
