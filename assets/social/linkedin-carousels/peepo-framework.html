1/8
P

People

Physical State

  • Fatigue / sleep
  • Illness / injury
  • Impairment

Mental State

  • Stress / pressure
  • Distraction
  • Complacency

Knowledge

  • Training adequacy
  • Experience level
  • Hazard awareness

Communication

  • Instructions clear?
  • Language barriers?
  • Handover adequate?
💡 Key Insight

People factors explain HOW an incident happened — but rarely WHY it was possible. Always dig deeper.

2/8
E

Environment

Physical Conditions

  • Weather
  • Lighting
  • Noise / temperature

Workspace

  • Housekeeping
  • Layout / access
  • Ground conditions

Temporal

  • Time of day
  • Shift patterns
  • End of shift rush

Location

  • Remote / isolated
  • Multi-employer site
  • Public interface
💡 Key Insight

Even the most skilled worker can fail in a hostile environment. The environment sets the stage.

3/8
E

Equipment

Design

  • Suitable for task?
  • Ergonomic?
  • Fails safely?

Condition

  • Maintenance status
  • Known defects?
  • Pre-use checks done?

Safety Features

  • Guards in place?
  • Interlocks working?
  • E-stops accessible?

PPE

  • Available?
  • Good condition?
  • Correct for hazard?
💡 Key Insight

Equipment doesn't maintain itself. Ask: who decided on maintenance schedules and budgets?

4/8
P

Procedures

Availability

  • Does it exist?
  • Accessible?
  • Right format?

Quality

  • Accurate?
  • Complete?
  • Up to date?

Practicality

  • Can it be followed?
  • Time to follow it?
  • Resources available?

Compliance

  • Workers know it?
  • Trained on it?
  • Actually followed?
💡 Key Insight

If workers routinely deviate from procedures, the procedure is the problem — not the workers.

5/8
O

Organization

Management Systems

  • Risk assessment
  • Change management
  • Permits / audits

Resources

  • Staffing levels
  • Budget allocation
  • Time / schedules

Culture

  • Safety priority
  • Reporting culture
  • Just culture

Competing Pressures

  • Production vs safety
  • Cost pressure
  • Schedule pressure
💡 This is where root causes live

Every P-E-E-P factor traces back to an organizational decision. Don't stop until you find it.
