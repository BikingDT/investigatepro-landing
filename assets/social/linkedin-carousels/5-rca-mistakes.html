5 Root Cause
Analysis Mistakes

That keep your incidents coming back

Swipe to learn
1/7
#1

Stopping at "Human Error"

❌ The Mistake

"Root cause: Worker failed to follow procedure"

✓ Instead Ask

WHY was it possible for them to fail? Was the procedure practical? Was there pressure to shortcut?

2/7
#2

Weak Corrective Actions

❌ The Mistake

"Retrain all workers" or "Add more warning signs"

✓ Instead

Use the Hierarchy of Controls. Start with elimination and engineering — not just admin controls that rely on human behavior.

3/7
#3

Confirmation Bias

❌ The Mistake

Forming conclusions early, then gathering evidence that confirms your theory

✓ Instead

Deliberately seek disconfirming evidence. Ask: "What would prove my theory wrong?"

4/7
#4

Not Reaching Organization

❌ The Mistake

Stopping at equipment failure or procedural gaps without asking WHY

✓ Instead Ask

"What management decision or system allowed this condition to exist?" That's where root causes live.

5/7
#5

Investigating in Isolation

❌ The Mistake

Treating each incident as unique. Filing and forgetting.

✓ Instead

Look for patterns across incidents. The same organizational factors often appear again and again.
