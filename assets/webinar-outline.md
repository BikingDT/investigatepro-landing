### SECTION 2: THE FRAMEWORK (15 min)

### Slide 8: Enter ICAM
- Incident Cause Analysis Method
- Developed for high-reliability industries
- Systematic approach to root cause identification
- Focus on organizational factors, not blame

"Not new. Just rarely implemented properly."

### Slide 9: The PEEPO Framework
Graphic: Pentagon with P-E-E-P-O

- **P**eople - Human factors
- **E**nvironment - Physical conditions
- **E**quipment - Tools and machinery
- **P**rocedures - Documented vs actual work
- **O**rganization - Where root causes live

"This ensures you consider every factor. Every time."

### Slide 10: P - People Factors
What to look for:
- Fatigue, stress, distraction
- Training and competency
- Experience level
- Physical/cognitive state

"Important: These aren't ROOT causes. They're symptoms of organizational factors."

### Slide 11: E - Environment Factors
What to look for:
- Lighting, noise, temperature
- Workspace layout
- Housekeeping
- Weather conditions

"Did the environment set people up to succeed or fail?"

### Slide 12: E - Equipment Factors
What to look for:
- Design adequacy
- Maintenance state
- Guarding effectiveness
- Ergonomics

"Is this the right tool for the job? Is it in the right condition?"

### Slide 13: P - Procedure Factors
What to look for:
- Does a procedure exist?
- Is it physically possible to follow?
- Is it known and accessible?
- Do people actually follow it?

"The gap between 'work as imagined' and 'work as done' is where incidents live."

### Slide 14: O - Organizational Factors (The Key)
Every P-E-E-P factor exists because of an organizational decision:

- People exhausted → Scheduling decision
- Environment hazardous → Investment decision
- Equipment inadequate → Procurement decision
- Procedure impossible → Design decision

"This is where root causes live. This is where you can make change."

### Slide 15: The 5 Whys in Practice
Example walkthrough:

- Worker rushed → WHY?
- Time pressure → WHY?
- Understaffed → WHY?
- Budget cuts → WHY?
- Wrong metrics incentivizing cost over safety

"Keep asking until you reach a decision someone made."

### Slide 16: Hierarchy of Controls
Graphic: Triangle/Pyramid

- Elimination (most effective)
- Substitution
- Engineering controls
- Administrative controls
- PPE (least effective)

"Push for higher tiers. 'Retrain workers' should be the last resort, not the first."
