# Demo Call Script - InvestigatePro

**Duration:** 30 minutes (15 min discovery, 12 min demo, 3 min close)

---

## Pre-Call Prep (5 min before)

- [ ] Review LinkedIn (their role, tenure, posts)
- [ ] Check company (industry, size, recent news)
- [ ] Look for recent incidents (news search)
- [ ] Open InvestigatePro demo environment
- [ ] Have ROI calculator ready

---

## Opening (2 min)

**Goal:** Build rapport, set agenda, get buy-in

---

"Hi [Name], thanks for taking the time today. I'm [Your name] from InvestigatePro.

Before we dive in, I'd love to make sure this is valuable for you. Here's what I was thinking:

1. First, I'd like to learn about your current investigation process and what prompted you to look at this
2. Then I'll show you how InvestigatePro works — specifically the parts relevant to what you shared
3. We'll wrap up with next steps if it looks like a fit

Sound good? Anything you'd like to add to that agenda?"

---

## Discovery (12-15 min)

**Goal:** Understand their pain, qualify, set up the demo

---

### Current State Questions

**"Tell me about your current investigation process."**
- Listen for: methodology (or lack of), tools used, who investigates
- Follow-up: "Walk me through what happens when an incident occurs."

**"How long does a typical investigation take from incident to closed?"**
- Listen for: days/weeks, bottlenecks, frustration
- Note the number for ROI calculation

**"Who's responsible for conducting investigations?"**
- Listen for: dedicated safety staff vs supervisors, training level
- Follow-up: "How consistent is the quality across investigators?"

### Pain Questions

**"What's working well with your current process?"**
- Let them share positives (builds trust)
- Listen for gaps they don't mention

**"If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about your investigations, what would it be?"**
- This is their main pain point
- Write it down verbatim

**"How often do you see similar incidents recurring?"**
- Listen for: repeat rate, frustration, specific examples
- Follow-up: "What do you think is causing that?"

### Impact Questions

**"What happens when investigations don't find the real root cause?"**
- Listen for: repeat incidents, regulatory issues, leadership pressure
- Connect to business impact

**"How does this affect you personally?"**
- Listen for: stress, time pressure, accountability
- Build emotional connection

### Decision Questions

**"What prompted you to look at this now?"**
- Listen for: trigger event, leadership mandate, upcoming audit
- Understand urgency

**"Besides yourself, who else would be involved in this decision?"**
- Map the buying committee
- Ask: "What matters most to them?"

**"What would success look like for you?"**
- Define their success criteria
- Use their words back in the demo

---

## Transition to Demo

**"Thanks for sharing all that. Based on what you've told me, here's what I think would be most valuable to show you:**

**[Customize based on discovery]**

- If they mentioned inconsistency: "How we ensure every investigation follows the same methodology"
- If they mentioned repeat incidents: "How we trace findings to organizational root causes"
- If they mentioned time: "How we cut investigation time by 60%"
- If they mentioned compliance: "How reports are automatically generated for regulators"

"Let me show you how this works in practice."

---

## Demo (12 min)

**Goal:** Show, don't tell. Connect features to their pain.

---

### Demo Flow

**1. Start with an Incident (2 min)**
"Let's say we just had an incident — a hand injury during maintenance."

- Show incident creation
- Highlight mobile capability
- Note: "Your supervisors can start this immediately from the floor"

**2. PEEPO Analysis (4 min)**
"This is where InvestigatePro is different. Instead of a blank form, we guide investigators through ICAM methodology."

- Walk through each PEEPO category
- Show AI coaching prompts
- Highlight: "See how it's asking questions they might not think to ask?"

**3. Organizational Factors (3 min)**
"This is the key — we don't let investigations stop at human error."

- Show how findings trace to organizational factors
- Demonstrate the "Why" prompts
- Connect: "This is what prevents repeat incidents — fixing systems, not blaming people"

**4. Corrective Actions (2 min)**
"Once we identify root causes, we assign corrective actions with accountability."

- Show Hierarchy of Controls guidance
- Show assignment and tracking
- Highlight: "Leadership can see exactly what's pending and who's responsible"

**5. Pattern Recognition (1 min)**
"And here's where it gets powerful over time..."

- Show cross-incident patterns
- Highlight: "This is how you answer 'have we seen this before?'"

---

### During Demo

**Tie everything back to their words:**
- "You mentioned [their pain]. This is how we address that..."
- "Remember when you said [their goal]? Watch this..."

**Ask engagement questions:**
- "Can you see how this would work for your team?"
- "Is this the level of detail you'd need?"
- "What questions does this raise for you?"

**Handle objections in real-time:**
- "That's a great question. Let me show you..."
- "I hear that concern. Here's how other [industry] companies handle it..."

---

## Close (3 min)

**Goal:** Confirm fit, establish next steps, create urgency

---

### Temperature Check

"Based on what you've seen, how does this compare to what you were hoping for?"

- Listen for buying signals or concerns
- Address any final objections

### Recap Value

"So to summarize what we covered:
- [Feature] addresses your concern about [pain #1]
- [Feature] solves [pain #2]
- And [Feature] helps you achieve [their success criteria]"

### Create Urgency

"You mentioned [trigger/timeline]. To hit that, we'd want to start [timeframe].

We're offering founding customers:
- 3 months free
- White-glove onboarding
- Direct line to our team for feature requests"

### Establish Next Steps

"What would need to happen for you to move forward?"

Listen, then propose concrete next step:
- "Let's schedule a call with [other stakeholder] for [specific date]"
- "I'll send over a proposal by [date]. Can we book time [date] to review?"
- "Want to start a pilot with [specific team/site]?"

### Confirm

"Great, so our next step is [action] by [date]. I'll send a calendar invite right after this call."

---

## Post-Call Actions

- [ ] Send thank you email within 1 hour
- [ ] Include: recording (if applicable), relevant resources, proposal/pricing
- [ ] Send calendar invite for next meeting
- [ ] Update CRM with notes
- [ ] Set follow-up reminder if no response in 48h

---

## Objection Handling

### "We need to think about it."
"Totally understand. What specifically would you be thinking through?"
→ Identify the real concern, address it

### "It's not in the budget."
"I hear you. Let me share some quick math..."
→ ROI calculator: show cost of repeat incidents vs InvestigatePro cost

### "We need to talk to other stakeholders."
"Of course. Would it help if I joined that conversation to answer questions directly?"
→ Get invited to the next meeting

### "We're looking at other options."
"Smart to evaluate alternatives. What criteria matter most to you?"
→ Position against competitors, schedule follow-up after their evaluations

### "Implementation seems complex."
"Good concern. Most customers are live in 2-3 weeks. We handle the heavy lifting."
→ Offer to connect with reference customer

### "Our investigators won't use it."
"Change is hard. Here's what we've seen work..."
→ Share adoption success stories, offer pilot approach

## Discovery Questions Bank

**Current State:**
- How many incidents do you typically investigate per year?
- What methodology do you use today?
- Where do investigations get documented?
- How do you track corrective actions?

**Pain:**
- What's the biggest frustration with your current process?
- How often do you see similar incidents repeat?
- What happens when investigations don't find root causes?
- How much time do investigations take?

**Impact:**
- What does a repeat incident cost you?
- How does investigation quality affect your regulatory standing?
- What would better investigations mean for your team?

**Decision:**
- What's driving the timing on this?
- Who else would be involved in this decision?
- What's your timeline for making a change?
- What would success look like?
