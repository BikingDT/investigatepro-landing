## Week 5: Practical Tool

**Subject Lines:**
- A: "The questions most investigators forget to ask"
- B: "Witness interview cheat sheet"
- C: "Get better answers (without blame)"

**Body:**

Hi {{FirstName}},

The quality of your investigation depends on the quality of your questions.

Most investigators ask:
- "What happened?"
- "Why did you do that?"
- "Didn't you know the procedure?"

These questions get defensive answers. Or no answers at all.

Better questions:

**Instead of "Why did you...?"**
→ "What factors influenced your decision?"
→ "Walk me through your thinking at that moment."

**Instead of "Didn't you know...?"**
→ "How were you trained on this?"
→ "What's the typical way this task gets done?"

**Instead of "Who's responsible?"**
→ "What resources would have helped?"
→ "If you could change one thing about the system, what would it be?"

People open up when they feel safe. And when they open up, you find the real root causes.
