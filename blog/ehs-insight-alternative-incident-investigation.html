EHS Insight Alternative for Incident Investigation (2026)

EHS Insight is a widely used mid-market EHS platform that handles a lot of the basics well. But for teams in high-hazard industries where incident investigation quality — not just incident recording — is the priority, its limitations become apparent quickly. Here's an honest comparison of where EHS Insight works, where it falls short, and what teams are switching to.

What Is EHS Insight?

EHS Insight is a cloud-based environmental, health, and safety management platform targeting mid-market organisations — typically companies too large for spreadsheets and basic forms, but not large enough to justify the enterprise price tag of Cority or Intelex. It offers modules for incident management, audits, training, inspections, risk assessments, and compliance tracking.

Its pricing model is more accessible than the enterprise tier — typically in the $5,000–$20,000/year range — and it deploys faster than the big enterprise platforms. For general EHS management, it's a reasonable choice for companies in the 200–2,000 employee range.

So why are teams looking for alternatives? The answer usually comes down to one thing: investigation depth.

The Core Problem with EHS Insight for Incident Investigation

EHS Insight, like most general-purpose EHS platforms, treats incident investigation as a form-filling exercise. You capture what happened, who was involved, what immediate actions were taken, and what corrective actions are assigned. That's incident recording — and it's not the same as incident investigation.

Genuine incident investigation — the kind that prevents recurrence — requires a structured methodology that guides investigators to look beyond the immediate causes and explore the systemic contributing factors: task design, equipment condition, team communication, supervisory decisions, organisational pressures. Without that structure, investigations conclude with "human error" or "failure to follow procedure," corrective actions address symptoms rather than causes, and incidents recur.

EHS Insight doesn't provide that structure. It gives you a form. What you do with it is up to you.

Why Teams Look for an EHS Insight Alternative

Pain #1: No built-in investigation methodology

EHS Insight's incident module captures event data and basic root cause categories — but it doesn't guide investigators through ICAM, PEEPO, Bow Tie, or any other structured methodology. If your organisation uses ICAM (common in mining, energy, and resources), you're working around the tool, not with it. Your investigators are filling out a generic form while your methodology lives in a separate document — if it's used at all.

Pain #2: Root cause analysis is a dropdown, not a workflow

EHS Insight typically provides a configurable list of root cause categories. Investigators select from the list. That's not root cause analysis — that's categorisation. Real RCA means asking "why" multiple times, exploring contributing factors systematically, and challenging initial conclusions. A dropdown menu doesn't do that. Neither does any EHS Insight configuration out of the box.

Pain #3: No AI coaching for investigators

EHS Insight doesn't provide AI-assisted investigation guidance. There's no system that prompts investigators when they may be missing contributing factors, challenges a conclusion that looks superficial, or helps a less experienced investigator understand what questions to ask next. For organisations where investigators are supervisors or safety advisors — not full-time investigators — that gap in guided support translates directly into investigation quality.

Pain #4: Designed for EHS managers, not field investigators

EHS Insight's interface is built for EHS professionals managing compliance from an office. It's not designed for a mine site supervisor conducting an investigation on a tablet in the field, or an engineering team leader walking through a post-incident analysis on a construction site. The mobile experience is functional but not optimised for the field workflows that matter most in high-hazard industries.

Pain #5: Still requires significant setup for high-hazard use cases

While EHS Insight deploys faster than enterprise platforms, getting it configured for serious investigation use — custom workflow steps, tailored root cause taxonomies, ICAM-aligned factor categories — still requires meaningful setup time and often professional services. For teams that need a tool working in days, not weeks, that's a friction point.

When EHS Insight Is Still the Right Choice

To be balanced: EHS Insight is a solid mid-market platform for the right use case. It makes sense when:

  • You need a unified EHS platform covering incidents, audits, inspections, training, and risk assessments in one system
  • Your investigations are relatively straightforward — slip/trip/falls, minor injuries — where a structured methodology like ICAM isn't contractually required or operationally critical
  • Your primary challenge is compliance documentation and audit readiness rather than investigation depth
  • You're in a lower-hazard industry (office environments, retail, light manufacturing) where the cost of poor investigations is lower
  • You need training management and inspection workflows alongside incident recording

If your organisation fits that profile, EHS Insight is a reasonable tool at a reasonable price. The issue is when teams with serious investigation requirements — mining, construction, oil & gas, energy, heavy engineering — find themselves using a compliance platform for a job it was never designed to do.

The Investigation Methodology Gap in Practice

Here's what the absence of built-in methodology actually looks like in practice:

A mining contractor uses EHS Insight for their incident management. After a serious near-miss — a vehicle rollover on a haul road — the site safety advisor opens an incident record, fills in the event details, selects "vehicle operations" from the incident category dropdown, adds "driver failed to control speed on descent" as the immediate cause, selects "human factors" from the root cause dropdown, and assigns a corrective action: "Retrain all haul truck operators."

Two months later, a similar incident occurs. The road gradient hadn't been reassessed. The shift handover process hadn't flagged the ongoing risk. The supervisor hadn't intervened when speed violations were observed in the weeks prior. None of these contributing factors appeared in the EHS Insight record — not because the investigator was negligent, but because the tool never prompted them to look.

This is the investigation quality gap. EHS Insight captured what happened. InvestigatePro would have guided the investigator to understand why — systematically, across all the contributing factor categories that ICAM identifies: task demands, individual factors, team factors, equipment/environment, and organisational factors. The corrective actions would have been fundamentally different, and the recurrence risk would have been significantly lower.
