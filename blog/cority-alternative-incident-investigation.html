Cority Alternative for Incident Investigation (2026)

Cority is one of the most capable enterprise EHS platforms on the market — but for teams where incident investigation quality is the primary challenge, it's often the wrong tool at the wrong price. Here's an honest look at where Cority excels, where it falls short for investigation-focused teams, and what high-hazard industries are switching to.

What Is Cority, and Who Is It Built For?

Cority (formerly Medgate) is a cloud-based enterprise EHS management platform used primarily by large corporations in manufacturing, pharmaceuticals, energy, and resources. Its core strengths are occupational health management, industrial hygiene, environmental compliance, and broad EHSQ (Environment, Health, Safety, Quality) administration.

It's a serious platform — used by Fortune 500 companies, government agencies, and regulated industries that need to manage complex compliance obligations across thousands of workers in multiple jurisdictions. For those organisations, it delivers real value.

But Cority is designed for a specific customer: the large enterprise with a dedicated EHS team, a multi-year implementation budget, and compliance complexity that justifies its cost and configuration overhead. If that doesn't describe your situation — and for many mid-market mining, construction, and energy operations, it doesn't — you're likely paying for a lot you'll never use while missing depth in the one area you actually need: structured incident investigation.

Why Teams Look for a Cority Alternative

Pain #1: Enterprise pricing with enterprise complexity

Cority's contracts start at $20,000–$40,000/year and scale well above $100,000 for larger deployments, plus implementation and professional services fees. For a 100–400 person mining contractor or construction firm, this pricing tier simply isn't justifiable for a safety investigation tool — especially when investigation is the primary workflow, not a module in a broader compliance program.

Pain #2: Implementation takes quarters, not days

Cority implementations are measured in months to years. You're not buying software you can turn on — you're buying a project. A typical SMB deployment requires 6–12 months of configuration, training, and data migration before investigators can actually use it productively. If you're trying to improve investigation quality after a serious incident, that timeline is a problem.

Pain #3: Incident investigation is one module among many

Cority's incident module is a part of its broader platform — it captures incident data, generates reports, and tracks corrective actions. But it doesn't guide investigators through structured methodologies like ICAM or PEEPO. Root cause analysis in Cority is typically a configurable dropdown menu, not a coached workflow. For organisations where investigation quality matters — where "human error" conclusions are a sign of failure, not analysis — this isn't enough.

Pain #4: Field usability is an afterthought

Cority is primarily a desktop platform designed for EHS managers in offices, not investigators on a mine site, drilling platform, or construction site. Mobile access exists, but it's a view of a desktop system, not a mobile-first experience. For field teams collecting evidence in real-time during an investigation, this creates friction that affects investigation quality.

Pain #5: No AI investigation coaching

Cority does not offer AI-guided investigation support. There is no system that prompts investigators to explore contributing factors they've missed, challenges superficial conclusions, or guides less-experienced investigators through structured root cause analysis. That gap is significant when investigation quality — not just compliance documentation — is the goal.

When Cority Is Actually the Right Choice

Before we go further, it's worth being clear: Cority is a strong platform for the right customer. It makes sense when:

  • You have 1,000+ employees with complex multi-site EHS obligations
  • You need a unified platform spanning occupational health, industrial hygiene, environmental monitoring, audits, and incidents
  • You operate in regulated industries (pharma, aerospace, utilities) requiring detailed compliance trails and regulatory reporting
  • You have a dedicated EHS team with time and budget to configure and maintain a complex platform
  • You need enterprise integrations with HRIS, SAP, Oracle, or other enterprise systems
  • Your primary challenge is compliance documentation and reporting, not investigation methodology improvement

If all of this describes your organisation, Cority earns its price tag. For everyone else, you're likely overpaying for complexity you don't need.

The Investigation Quality Gap

This is the most important comparison, and it's not about features — it's about outcomes.

Cority's incident module will help you document that an investigation happened. It will capture the date, the people involved, a description of the event, and a set of corrective actions. What it won't do is help your investigators actually investigate — to systematically identify the contributing factors, challenge superficial conclusions, and understand the systemic causes that, if left unaddressed, will lead to the next incident.

This matters because the most common failure in incident investigation isn't a lack of documentation — it's a lack of depth. Investigations that conclude with "operator error" or "failure to follow procedure" without exploring why those failures occurred generate corrective actions that don't address root causes. And incidents recur.

The ICAM difference: ICAM (Incident Cause Analysis Method) was developed specifically to address this problem. It provides a structured framework for exploring contributing factors across people, environment, equipment, procedures, and organisation — making it significantly harder for investigators to reach superficial conclusions. InvestigatePro has ICAM built into every investigation, not as a configurable option, but as the guided workflow every investigator follows from step one.

No amount of Cority configuration replicates a methodology that's genuinely embedded in the tool's design. You can build forms that capture ICAM-like data — but you can't configure a tool to challenge an investigator's thinking in real time the way AI coaching does.
