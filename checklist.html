25 Essential Steps for Thorough Root Cause Analysis

1 Initial Response (First 30 Minutes)

Secure the scene ‚Äî Prevent further harm and preserve evidence
Ensure medical attention ‚Äî Confirm all injured persons are receiving care
Notify relevant parties ‚Äî Supervisors, safety team, management as required
Document initial conditions ‚Äî Photos, videos, sketches before anything changes
Identify witnesses ‚Äî List everyone present before memories fade
üí° Pro Tip:

Take photos from multiple angles and distances. What seems obvious now may be crucial later.

2 Evidence Collection

Collect physical evidence ‚Äî Equipment, materials, PPE, environmental samples
Gather documentation ‚Äî Work orders, procedures, training records, maintenance logs
Review CCTV/recordings ‚Äî Security cameras, dashcams, wearable devices
Download equipment data ‚Äî Black boxes, telemetry, sensor logs
Map the scene ‚Äî Create detailed diagram with measurements

3 Witness Interviews

Interview separately ‚Äî Prevent influence between witnesses
Use open questions ‚Äî "Tell me what happened" not "Did X cause this?"
Document verbatim quotes ‚Äî Exact words matter for accuracy
Explore the timeline ‚Äî Build minute-by-minute sequence of events
Ask about "normal" operations ‚Äî Understand if this was typical or different
üí° Pro Tip:

Never ask "Why did you...?" ‚Äî it triggers defensiveness. Ask "What was happening when...?" instead.

4 ICAM Analysis

Identify Absent/Failed Defences ‚Äî What barriers should have prevented harm?
List Individual/Team Actions ‚Äî Actions and decisions (without blame)
Identify Task/Environmental Conditions ‚Äî Context that shaped decisions
Uncover Organisational Factors ‚Äî Systems, culture, resources that contributed
Apply the 5 Whys technique ‚Äî Dig deeper on each contributing factor

5 Corrective Actions & Close-out

Develop SMART actions ‚Äî Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound
Use the hierarchy of controls ‚Äî Elimination > Substitution > Engineering > Admin > PPE
Assign clear ownership ‚Äî One person accountable per action item
Share learnings widely ‚Äî Safety alerts, toolbox talks, cross-site communication
Verify effectiveness ‚Äî Follow up to confirm actions prevent recurrence
üí° Pro Tip:

60% of incidents have organizational root causes. If all your actions are "retraining," dig deeper.
