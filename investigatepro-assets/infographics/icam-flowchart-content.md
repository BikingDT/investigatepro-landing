# The Complete ICAM Investigation Method

## What is ICAM?
ICAM = Incident Cause Analysis Method
A structured approach to investigating workplace incidents that goes beyond blame to find systemic causes.

## The 5 Stages of ICAM Investigation

### Stage 1: Data Collection
- Gather physical evidence
- Interview witnesses
- Review documents & records
- Photograph the scene
- Preserve equipment

### Stage 2: Organize Data
- Create timeline of events
- Map out involved parties
- Identify equipment involved
- Document environmental conditions
- Categorize information

### Stage 3: Identify Contributing Factors
Four Categories:
1. **Absent/Failed Defenses** - Safety barriers that didn't work
2. **Individual Actions** - Human errors or violations
3. **Task/Environmental Conditions** - Workplace pressures
4. **Organizational Factors** - Management system gaps

### Stage 4: Determine Root Causes
- Ask "Why?" repeatedly (5 Whys technique)
- Trace factors back to organizational systems
- Identify management system failures
- Look for patterns across incidents

### Stage 5: Develop Recommendations
- Address root causes, not symptoms
- Make recommendations SMART:
  - Specific
  - Measurable
  - Achievable
  - Relevant
  - Time-bound
- Prioritize by risk reduction impact

## Key Principle
"No blame culture" - Focus on fixing systems, not punishing people

## Benefits of ICAM
✓ Reduces repeat incidents
✓ Improves safety culture
✓ Identifies systemic weaknesses
✓ Creates actionable improvements
✓ Builds organizational learning
