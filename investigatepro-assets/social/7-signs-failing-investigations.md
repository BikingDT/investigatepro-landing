## Slide 2 - Sign #1
**#1: Same Incidents Keep Happening**
If you're investigating similar incidents repeatedly, you're treating symptoms, not root causes.

🔴 Red Flag: "Didn't we have this same issue last month?"

## Slide 3 - Sign #2  
**#2: Recommendations Gather Dust**
Investigations produce reports that sit in folders. No one follows up. Nothing changes.

🔴 Red Flag: "We wrote 50 recommendations last year. How many were implemented?"

## Slide 4 - Sign #3
**#3: It Always Ends With "Human Error"**
Blaming the worker is easy. It's also useless. Systems create the conditions for errors.

🔴 Red Flag: "The worker should have been more careful"

## Slide 5 - Sign #4
**#4: Investigations Take Forever**
Weeks or months to complete. By then, witnesses forget, evidence disappears, urgency fades.

🔴 Red Flag: "We're still working on that incident from 3 months ago"

## Slide 6 - Sign #5
**#5: Only Serious Incidents Get Investigated**
Near-misses are gold mines of learning. If you only investigate injuries, you're missing 90% of the insights.

🔴 Red Flag: "It was just a near-miss, no need to investigate"

## Slide 7 - Sign #6
**#6: Investigators Aren't Trained**
Good intentions don't replace methodology. ICAM, 5-Whys, Root Cause Analysis - these are skills that need training.

🔴 Red Flag: "Just write up what happened"

## Slide 8 - Sign #7
**#7: Leadership Doesn't Review Results**
If senior leaders never see investigation findings, safety isn't really a priority.

🔴 Red Flag: "Does the CEO even know about our investigation program?"
