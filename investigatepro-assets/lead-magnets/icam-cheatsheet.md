## Page 1: What is ICAM?

**ICAM = Incident Cause Analysis Method**

A structured, systematic approach to investigating incidents that focuses on:
- Understanding WHAT happened
- Identifying WHY it happened  
- Preventing it from happening AGAIN

**Key Principle:** No blame - focus on systems, not people

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                ORGANIZATIONAL FACTORS                │

├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤

                    INCIDENT
```

## Page 3: The 5-Stage Process

| Stage | Actions | Key Questions |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| **1. DATA COLLECTION** | Gather evidence, interview witnesses, photograph scene | What happened? Who was involved? |
| **2. ORGANIZE DATA** | Create timeline, categorize info | In what sequence? Under what conditions? |
| **3. IDENTIFY FACTORS** | Use the 4 categories framework | Why did each action/condition occur? |
| **4. ROOT CAUSES** | 5-Whys analysis, trace to systems | What organizational factors enabled this? |
| **5. RECOMMENDATIONS** | SMART actions targeting root causes | What system changes will prevent recurrence? |

## Page 4: Quick Reference - Contributing Factors

### Absent/Failed Defenses
- Inadequate PPE
- Missing guards/barriers
- Faulty warning systems
- Insufficient supervision
- Inadequate training verification

### Individual/Team Actions
- Errors (slips, lapses, mistakes)
- Violations (routine, situational, exceptional)
- Communication failures
- Inadequate risk assessment

### Task/Environmental Conditions
- Time pressure
- Inadequate tools/equipment
- Poor housekeeping
- Weather/visibility
- Fatigue/workload

### Organizational Factors
- Inadequate policies/procedures
- Conflicting goals (production vs safety)
- Poor communication channels
- Inadequate resources
- Weak safety culture

## Page 5: The 5-Whys Technique

**Example:**
- **Problem:** Worker injured hand
- **Why 1?** Guard was removed
- **Why 2?** Guard interfered with task
- **Why 3?** Guard not designed for this machine variant
- **Why 4?** No engineering review for machine modification
- **Why 5?** No Management of Change process

**ROOT CAUSE:** Missing Management of Change procedure

## Page 6: Writing SMART Recommendations

| Element | Question | Example |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **S**pecific | What exactly will be done? | "Install light curtain on press #3" |
| **M**easurable | How will we know it's done? | "Verified by safety inspection" |
| **A**chievable | Can we actually do this? | "Within maintenance budget" |
| **R**elevant | Does it address root cause? | "Prevents access to danger zone" |
| **T**ime-bound | By when? | "Complete by March 31" |

## Page 7: Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Stopping at "human error"
❌ Accepting first explanation
❌ Skipping witness interviews
❌ Focusing only on injured person
❌ Recommending "more training" as only solution
❌ Not following up on recommendations
