The Problem

Why Construction Investigations Are Harder Than They Should Be

Construction sites are dynamic, multi-employer environments. Standard investigation tools weren't built for this complexity.

🏗️

Dozens of Contractors, One Incident

When a subcontractor worker is injured on your site, who owns the investigation? Accountability gaps between principal and sub-contractor create delays, defensiveness, and shallow findings.

⏱️

Deadline Pressure Cuts Investigation Short

On projects with tight schedules, there's always pressure to get back to work quickly. Investigations get rushed. Root causes go unidentified. Conditions that caused the incident persist.

📱

Investigators Aren't at a Desk

Site safety managers are on the ground. Paper forms get lost. Emails don't capture the evidence chain. Investigation data ends up scattered across WhatsApp messages and shared drives.

📋

Paper Forms That Stop at Human Error

Generic investigation forms encourage investigators to identify "who did what wrong" rather than the management system failures that set the stage for the incident.

⚖️

Regulator Requirements Are Tightening

Construction fatality investigations increasingly require evidence of systemic causal analysis. A one-page "worker didn't follow procedure" report no longer satisfies regulators or owners.

🔁

The Same Incidents Keep Happening

Without a system that tracks corrective actions to completion and surfaces trends across projects, the same fall-from-height and struck-by incidents recur project after project.

Hazard Coverage

Built for Construction's Leading Causes of Harm

InvestigatePro's ICAM framework is calibrated for the incident types most common on construction and civil works projects.

🪜

Falls from Height

Scaffold failures, edge protection gaps, ladder incidents. ICAM analysis surfaces fall prevention system design, inspection frequency, permit-to-work quality, and supervision practices.

🦺

Struck-By & Caught-Between

Plant and pedestrian interaction, falling objects, crane operations. Investigation covers traffic management plans, exclusion zone systems, spotter protocols, and pre-lift planning.

⚡

Electrical Contact

Underground service strikes, overhead line contact, temporary power incidents. ICAM examines dial-before-you-dig compliance, service location systems, and electrical LOTO procedures.

🕳️

Excavation & Trenching

Cave-in, engulfment, and spoil pile instability events. Investigates soil assessment systems, shoring inspection regimes, confined space entry procedures, and atmospheric monitoring.

🔥

Hot Work & Fire

Welding, grinding, and cutting incidents. ICAM analysis covers hot work permit systems, fire watch procedures, combustible material management, and extinguisher placement.

🧱

Structural & Formwork Failure

Propping failures, formwork collapse, structural instability. Investigation examines design review processes, loadout sequencing, engineer-of-record involvement, and inspection sign-offs.

🤕

Musculoskeletal & Manual Handling

High-volume but often under-investigated. ICAM surfaces ergonomic design decisions, task rotation systems, and the production pressure that leads workers to skip mechanical aids.

🏢

Subcontractor Management Failures

Principal-contractor interface incidents. Captures pre-qualification standards, induction quality, supervision frequency, and the organizational pressure that leads to shortcuts.

Process

From Scene to Closed Investigation

A complete investigation workflow designed for the pace and complexity of active construction sites.

1

Report from Site

Log the incident immediately via mobile or desktop. Capture photos, GPS location, classification, personnel involved. Takes under 3 minutes — no excuses for delayed reporting.

2

Preserve Evidence

Guided evidence checklist ensures nothing is missed. Attach site photos, CCTV captures, equipment inspection records, toolbox talk records, and witness statements directly to the investigation.

3

Conduct ICAM Analysis

Structured ICAM workflow guides investigators through all four causal factor levels. AI coaching prompts deeper analysis when findings are shallow. Assign tasks to contractor investigators as needed.

4

Generate the Report

One click produces a complete, professionally formatted investigation report. Client-ready and regulator-ready. No additional formatting needed.

5

Close Out Actions

All recommendations are tracked as actions with owners, due dates, and escalation rules. The investigation doesn't close until every critical action is verified complete and evidence attached.
