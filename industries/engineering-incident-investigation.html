The Problem

Why Engineering Firms Struggle with Incident Investigation

Engineering project sites generate complex, multi-causal incidents. Most investigation tools aren't built for that complexity.

🏗️

Complex Sites, Multiple Contractors

Engineering projects involve principal contractors, specialist subcontractors, and client personnel on the same site. When an incident occurs, the chain of responsibility is complex — and shallow investigations miss the systemic coordination failures that caused it.

📋

Regulatory Scrutiny Is High

Engineering firms operate under CDM, OSHA, and industry-specific safety standards. Regulators and clients expect documented, methodologically sound investigations — not narrative reports that conclude "operative failed to follow procedure."

🔄

Lessons Don't Transfer Between Projects

A fall-from-height incident on a bridge project and a near-miss on a utilities upgrade may share identical systemic causes — but if investigations live in separate Word documents across project folders, the organisational learning never happens.

⏱️

Project Pressure Rushes Investigations

Engineering projects run on tight programmes. Every day of investigation is a day of delay. The pressure to close investigations quickly leads to surface-level findings and corrective actions that don't address the real systemic cause.

🌍

Multi-Country & Remote Site Challenges

Engineering firms operating in Africa, the Middle East, and resource-rich regions face investigations that span language barriers, limited internet, and varying regulatory environments. Paper-based systems are unworkable at scale.

📉

pSIF Events Go Unrecognised

Potential Serious Injury or Fatality (pSIF) events in engineering are often classified as minor near-misses. Without structured investigation, the systemic failures that nearly caused a fatality are never surfaced — until the next event is fatal.

Hazard Coverage

Built for Engineering's Most Critical Incident Types

Investigation guidance calibrated for the hazards that drive engineering fatality and serious injury rates globally.

🪜

Falls from Height

The leading cause of fatalities in engineering and construction. ICAM investigates fall protection system design, inspection regimes, permit-to-work quality, supervision adequacy, and the production pressure that leads workers to bypass protection.

🏗️

Crane & Lifting Operations

Load drops, rigging failures, and crane collisions on engineering sites. Investigation covers lift planning adequacy, rigger competency verification, exclusion zone management, communication systems, and equipment inspection regimes.

🌏

Excavation & Ground Collapse

Trench collapses, ground instability, and buried service strikes on civil and infrastructure projects. ICAM surfaces ground investigation adequacy, shoring system selection, permit-to-dig quality, and competency of trench supervisors.

⚡

Electrical & Utilities Strikes

Contact with live electrical cables, gas mains, and pressurised services. Investigation examines service detection systems, permit-to-work adequacy, subcontractor supervision, and the planning failures that put workers near unidentified services.

🚧

Struck-By & Vehicle Incidents

Pedestrian-vehicle interactions on construction sites and traffic management failures on road projects. ICAM investigates traffic management plan quality, segregation system implementation, contractor interface management, and supervisor oversight.

💧

Structural Failures & Collapses

Formwork collapses, structural failures during construction phases, and falling material incidents. Investigation examines design review processes, temporary works sign-off, competency of temporary works coordinators, and inspection systems.

🔥

Hot Work & Fire Incidents

Fire and explosion incidents involving welding, cutting, and hot work on engineering projects. ICAM surfaces hot work permit quality, fire watch adequacy, isolation systems, combustible material management, and contractor supervision.

🧪

Confined Space & Atmosphere Incidents

Atmospheric hazards in tunnels, chambers, and process vessels on infrastructure projects. Investigation examines atmosphere monitoring systems, rescue provisions, permit quality, and the decision-making process that allowed entry.
