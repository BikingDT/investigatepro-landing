The Problem

Why Manufacturing Incidents Keep Recurring

Manufacturing safety teams investigate hundreds of incidents a year — and watch many of the same types happen again. The investigation process is usually the problem.

🔄

Same Incidents, Different Shifts

A laceration on Line 3 is investigated, a corrective action is logged in a spreadsheet, and two months later the same incident happens on Line 7. Without systemic RCA and proper tracking, recurrence is inevitable.

🏃

Production Pressure Cuts Investigations Short

In manufacturing, every minute of downtime has a cost. Investigations get rushed. The first obvious cause becomes the finding. Organizational factors — machine design, work instructions, line speed targets — go unexamined.

📋

5 Whys Done Wrong

5 Whys is the most popular RCA technique in manufacturing — and one of the most poorly executed. Without structure and facilitation, investigations stop at "worker didn't follow procedure" instead of asking why the procedure was bypassed.

📁

Investigations Disappear into Shared Drives

Investigation reports sit in folder structures nobody searches. Lessons from a guarding incident in one facility never reach another. Corrective actions get assigned and forgotten. The organizational memory never builds.

📊

No Trend Analysis Across Lines or Sites

High incident rates on specific machines, shifts, or production lines go undetected because there's no system aggregating findings. Lagging indicators are only reviewed when the numbers become embarrassing.

⚖️

OSHA and Client Audits Demand More

Manufacturing clients and OSHA auditors increasingly ask for evidence of systematic causal analysis. A list of corrective actions without documented RCA methodology no longer satisfies a serious audit.

Hazard Coverage

Built for Manufacturing's Most Common Incident Types

Investigation prompts and causal factor categories calibrated for the hazards that drive manufacturing injury rates.

✂️

Cuts, Lacerations & Amputations

The most common manufacturing injury type. ICAM investigates guarding adequacy, maintenance bypass practices, work instruction clarity, and production pressure on safe work methods.

⚙️

Machine Guarding & Entanglement

Point-of-operation injuries, in-running nip points, and rotating equipment contact. Investigates guarding design, maintenance of protection systems, and bypass culture on production lines.

🔧

Lockout/Tagout Failures

Maintenance and servicing incidents where stored energy is released unexpectedly. ICAM surfaces LOTO procedure quality, verification steps, supervisor oversight, and training system adequacy.

💪

Musculoskeletal Injuries

High-volume, often under-investigated. ICAM examines ergonomic design decisions, rotation systems, task rate targets, and the production pressure that leads workers to skip rest protocols.

🪜

Slips, Trips & Falls

Housekeeping incidents, floor contamination, and working-at-height events. Investigation covers housekeeping systems, spill response procedures, footwear standards, and inspection regimes.

🏭

Forklift & Material Handling

Pedestrian strikes, tip-overs, and load-drop incidents. ICAM captures traffic management systems, operator competency verification, pre-shift inspection regimes, and warehouse layout design.

🔥

Chemical & Thermal Burns

Chemical exposure, steam burns, and molten material splatter. Investigates chemical management systems, PPE selection process, exposure monitoring, and work instruction adequacy for high-temperature tasks.

⚡

Electrical Safety

Arc flash, electrical contact, and shock incidents. ICAM analysis of electrical safety programs, arc flash assessment currency, qualified worker designation systems, and permit-to-work quality.
